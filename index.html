My Trip to Fire Island

A Summer Adventure on New York's Hidden Barrier Island

Fire Island National Seashore — where cars are banned and nature takes over.

Gallery: Fire Island

This summer I took a ferry from Bay Shore, New York, and spent four unforgettable days on Fire Island — a narrow barrier island off the south shore of Long Island. No roads, no cars, just boardwalks, ocean breeze, and the sound of the Atlantic waves. I explored the mysterious Sunken Forest, spotted wild deer wandering the paths, and watched sunsets paint the Great South Bay every evening. It was one of the most peaceful and beautiful places I have ever visited.
